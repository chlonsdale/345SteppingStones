Set the circumstances — timing, valuation, rates, rent, costs — and the tool works out the outcome: conventional, insured (and at what premium), or not financeable in full. Rules and market assumptions are editable — nothing is fixed except the Appendix B payment schedule.

Assumptions: (1) no CRA arrears — unresolved CRA debt blocks any application; (2) price fixed at $947,000, balances per Appendix B; (3) lenders use the lower of price and appraisal; (4) financing type is an outcome of LTV, not a choice; (5) planning figures only — a broker refines them. Method. Balance at application follows the Appendix B amortization schedule ($947,000 at 3%/yr calculated monthly, $6,500/month from 1 Sep 2025, first payment applied entirely to principal). A cash deposit entered for a scenario reduces the amount borrowed (balance − deposit) before anything else is calculated; its opportunity cost (what the cash could earn elsewhere) is not modelled. LTV = amount borrowed ÷ min(price, appraisal). Financing type is determined by LTV: at or under the conventional cap → conventional at the conventional rate, no premium; between the caps → insured at the insured rate, premium by the editable tier table (plus surcharge above 25-yr amortization), added to the loan; above the insured cap → maximum loan and cash shortfall shown. Qualifying rate = max(contract + buffer, floor). Income required: gross debt service (GDS) = (stress-tested P&I + property tax + heat − rent credit) ÷ GDS limit (GDS counts heat only, per the standard PITH definition — other utilities are excluded by lenders); total debt service (TDS) adds other debts; the binding (higher) figure is shown. Total family financing cost to horizon = vendor interest at 3% from Aug 2026 until application + insurance premium + bank interest from application to the comparison horizon + the rate spread Mum & Dad carry on their own mortgage while vendor financing continues (their rate minus the 3% received from Tim, on their approximate mortgage balance — held constant as a simplification — until discharge at completion). Interest, premium and spread only — principal builds equity either way.

Default rule values (accessed July 2026): OSFI minimum qualifying rate · CMHC premium tiers · FCAC down-payment rules · Ratehub current rates
